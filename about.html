Values I Believe In

My work is shaped as much by craft as by conviction. Especially when teams face ambiguity, chaos, or high stakes, I return to these principles as the foundation for judgment and action.

01

Disagree, then commit fully

I care deeply about quality so when I notice a problem or a misaligned direction, I'll say so honestly. But once the team has made a decision and the path is clear, I set aside my personal stance and push forward with full commitment. Real collaboration isn't about everyone agreeing on everything. It's about reaching alignment through honest discussion, then moving together toward the same goal.

02

Real teamwork

A great team isn't just about clear roles it's about genuine trust, honest communication, and resilience under pressure. They celebrate wins together and learn from failures without assigning blame. They support each other in hard moments instead of retreating into silos.

03

Be genuine

Kindness, honesty, and being yourself are the most important foundations for lasting collaboration. I'd rather work with someone reliable and real than someone skilled at surface impressions. Kindness isn't weakness, and honesty isn't offense they're the key to building trust, communicating clearly, and sustaining long term working relationships.
