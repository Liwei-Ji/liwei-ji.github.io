The Journey to Rebuild My Portfolio

For years, I’ve been searching for the best way to showcase my work and professional skills, but I’ve realized it’s time for a change. My journey started with Behance, a platform that is simple and easy to use, making it perfect for uploading design work. However, as my needs grew, I found that Behance could no longer meet them. It lacked the customization flexibility I needed, limiting my imagination on how to present my work.

Later, I tried using Notion to organize my portfolio. Notion is indeed a great tool for note-taking and organization, allowing me to present my creative journey in a more structured way. But it lacks interactivity, and the user experience is nowhere near that of a professional website. This made me realize: perhaps I need a truly personal online stage.

I wanted a platform that integrates all my resources, connecting professional articles and personal insights scattered across LinkedIn, Medium, and other platforms. Each of these platforms is great in its own way, but they are separate and don't fully represent my professional image. A unified website would allow visitors to get a more comprehensive understanding of me, offering a richer and more in-depth content experience.

More importantly, I hope the website evolves as I grow. It’s not just a static portfolio, but a reflection of my career journey, showcasing my commitment to continuous improvement. Each update represents another step forward in my professional development.

Therefore, the rebuilding process is not just a technical upgrade, but an investment in self-improvement, a journey towards excellence.

Disclaimer

    I’m not a professional front-end developer, but I’m really interested in learning and working on web development. I built this website to practice and improve my skills, so please keep in mind that I’m still learning as I go.
